# Human Before the Loop

Human-before-the-loop is the discipline of deciding what must remain possible before automation begins.

It is not the same as human-in-the-loop.

Human-in-the-loop usually means a person approves, corrects, or overrides an automated process that is already underway.

Human-before-the-loop means a person establishes the interpretive, institutional, and consequence-aware conditions under which automation may begin at all.

---

## The distinction

```text
Human-in-the-loop:
The system is moving. A human may approve or correct it.

Human-before-the-loop:
The system has not yet crossed the commitment boundary. A human defines what must remain possible after it acts.
```

The human is not present as an expensive rubber stamp for machine momentum. The human is there to frame the commitments that automation is allowed to make.

---

## Pairing with the kernel

```text
Human-before-the-loop asks:
What must remain possible?

The admissibility kernel asks:
Is this proposed commitment allowed without destroying it?
```

This pairing turns human judgement into a structured precondition for governed execution.
